At first glance, it seems that the legs are very simple in their structure: the front surface of the thigh (quadriceps), the rear surface of the thigh (biceps), buttocks and calf muscles. In reality, these are dozens of muscles, including not only pushing and pulling, but also stabilizers.

But think about it. You use your legs to stand, walk, run and balance. They serve as your foundation and are responsible for the transfer of all power from your body to the earth. When your legs are weak, the rest of your body should compensate for the work that your legs cannot do.

Be careful not to dive into the leg workout too quickly. You can create a problem that throws you back. Consider strengthening your legs as a long-term project. A little goes a long way, and you should gradually build it up with constant efforts and daily use.
